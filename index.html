Cultural/Religious Discrimination

Group 1

Introduction

What is cultural/religious discrimination all about?

Religious and cultural discrimination is the unjust treatment of people based on their religious beliefs or cultural practices. It can take many forms, including prejudice, violence, exclusion, and stereotyping. Prejudice frequently entails the unfair generalization and stereotyping of individuals from diverse cultural or religious backgrounds, leading to biased judgements. It may lead to exclusion from political, social, or economic opportunities, which would prevent full societal engagement.

Why there's a need to address it?

It is of utmost importance to tackle discrimination that is based on culture and religion as it violates basic human rights principles like equality and dignity. Moreover, it is essential to foster social harmony and reduce conflicts and animosity between different groups. The diversity that exists in society is a source of enrichment, whereas discrimination prevents us from realizing the advantages that come with it. Putting an end to discrimination has global benefits as it promotes tolerance, respect, and understanding among individuals from diverse backgrounds.
